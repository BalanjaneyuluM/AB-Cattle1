OUR BARNS



Let's begin with our largest facility, spanning an impressive 318,000 sq. ft.! This facility serves as the primary residence for
the majority of our cows, featuring an attached milking parlor equipped with a state-of-the-art 72-stall carousel.
Contrary to expectations, our cows genuinely appreciate this barn. Extensive research and over 130 years
of experience have guided us in understanding that cows thrive in a controlled environment.
Being creatures of habit, they find comfort in a predictable routine and appreciate the protection from
the unpredictable weather of New England.

Scientifically designed, this barn is geared towards fostering content and happy cows, ultimately resulting in the production
of high-quality milk. The facility is equipped with special curtains that can be adjusted to retain heat in the winter and
raised during the summer. Additionally, 180 adjustable fans across the back of the barn help maintain a comfortable temperature.
Following the model of our open-stall barns, the interior layout mirrors the exterior, allowing cows to freely roam,
huddle, rest, feed, or drink as they please.

The interior amenities include over 82 water troughs providing an unlimited supply of freshwater, ensuring the well-being of our cows.
Constant access to fresh feed and bedding further enhances their comfort. To ensure uninterrupted operations,
our barn is equipped with a 100% backup generator in case of power loss. Beyond our main barn, AB Cattle Inc.
extends this commitment to comfort and well-being to other facilities, such as our dry cow barn and calf barns, all designed to
provide the same exceptional level of care.

SUSTAINABILITY

While AB Cattle Inc. proudly upholds 120 years of cherished values and farming traditions, our facility stands as a testament
to modern, sustainable practices. In line with our commitment to the environment, our dry cow barn exemplifies
this ethos by being adorned with 752 solar panels, contributing to a remarkable 25% reduction in annual
electrical consumption.

When constructing our expansive 8-acre barn, we prioritized energy efficiency. We meticulously installed cutting-edge pumps,
fans, and lighting systems to minimize overall energy consumption. Moreover, our dedication to sustainability extends
to our manure management practices. As our cows undergo milking, a specialized manure
vacuum traverses the area, ensuring cleanliness.

The collected manure undergoes a sustainable process. In a designated room, it is deposited into a pit where the liquid component,
rich in nutrients, is separated from the dry solid fiber. The liquid waste is then pumped to a covered lagoon atop a hill, mitigating
odor and serving as a valuable fertilizer for cropland. Meanwhile, the dry solid fiber,a scentless
and nutrient-rich material resembling soft sawdust, is covered and stored for one year. Subsequently,
it is employed as bedding, changed daily to maintain optimal comfort for our cows.
